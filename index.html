About

A Lifelong Journey in Engineering and Service

I grew up in Rolpa, where early education was shaped by limited resources but fueled by big dreams. My academic journey led me through various Tribhuvan University institutions, culminating in a PhD in Geotechnical Engineering. Throughout my career, I have contributed to Nepal’s development by working on post-earthquake damage assessments, liquefaction studies, and numerous bridge projects, helping to rebuild and strengthen communities. Teaching and mentoring hundreds of aspiring engineers has been a rewarding part of my journey, allowing me to share knowledge and inspire the next generation.

My service in institutional roles has never been about titles or positions, but rather about creating opportunities and space for others to grow and lead. Each chapter of my story, from humble beginnings to academic and professional accomplishments, has been driven by a commitment to serve, uplift, and empower others, shaping both my personal growth and my contributions to the engineering field.

“The problems engineers face in Nepal are not technical alone — they are institutional, social, and systemic. That’s where I focus my energy.” — Er. Mandip Subedi

Institutional Leadership

Strengthening Engineering from Within

These roles were opportunities to listen, build platforms, and push for systems that protect and empower engineers — especially those working in difficult terrain, with limited support, and against time. Through collaborative leadership and advocacy, I aimed to create pathways for professional growth, encourage innovation, and ensure the voices of all engineers—both established and emerging—were heard and valued.
