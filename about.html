About

Er. Mandip Subedi Background, Expertise and Responsibilities

Mandip Subedi is a geotechnical engineer whose work spans the fault lines, floodplains, and fragile terrains of Nepal. Originally from Rolpa, his career has been grounded in translating scientific evidence into real-world impact—ensuring that the country’s infrastructure is prepared for the next disaster. From mapping liquefaction hazards in the Kathmandu Valley to conducting fieldwork after major earthquakes, Mandip’s work is rooted in both urgency and long-term resilience.

Following the devastating 3 November 2023 Jajarkot earthquake, Mandip led rapid reconnaissance missions that documented structural failures and geotechnical effects. These insights informed government agencies and engineers on safer rebuilding approaches, helping communities recover with resilience in mind. His earlier research, including probabilistic mapping of liquefaction risk using Kathmandu’s borehole data, has supported both local planning and national policy.

In engineering design, Mandip has been a key contributor to bridge foundation projects adapted to the challenging hydraulic and seismic conditions of the Himalayan region. Notably, he has worked on composite well-pile foundations—an innovative hybrid system tailored for riverine environments. His design philosophy prioritizes not just safety, but sustainability and adaptability, helping set new standards in Himalayan infrastructure development.

Beyond field and design work, Mandip is deeply committed to knowledge-sharing and professional capacity building. As President of the Nepal Geotechnical Society, he leads initiatives that promote research, collaboration, and technical training in geotechnical engineering. He is also an Associate Professor at Universal Engineering & Science College, where he mentors students, bridging theory with field-based problem-solving.

Through all these roles, Mandip Subedi remains driven by a singular goal: to ensure that engineering in Nepal doesn’t just respond to disasters, but anticipates them. His work continues to shape how Nepal understands and builds upon its complex and often unpredictable ground.
